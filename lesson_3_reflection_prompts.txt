rather than keeping all your work local?

It can allow you to collaborate with other people,
committing into the same remote, and to collaborate
with yourseld working from different stations,
comitting to same remote.

Q:
Describe the differences between forks, clones, and branches.
When would you use one instead of another?

A:
Fork is copying a GutHub repository to a new one.
Clone is copying a repository from a GitHub
or another source repository to a local place.
Branches are chains of commits within a repository.

Q:
What is the benefit of having a copy of the last known state
of the remote stored locally?

A:
You can adjust your changes to fit any conflicting changes
that happened on the remote after your previously pulled
data from it.
